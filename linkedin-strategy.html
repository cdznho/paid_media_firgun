Strategic posture

Build allocator gravity first.

Kris and Zeynep should use LinkedIn as a warm relationship system: visible expertise, account-specific engagement, and carefully timed private outreach. The goal is not broad reach. It is a short list of credible allocator conversations.

The motion should feel like investor relations, not sales development. Every public post should be educational and non-offer oriented. Every private message should be personalized, low-pressure, and anchored to a reason the allocator might care now: quantum timing, venture allocation fit, manager selection, governance, or event context.

  • Use named account lists. Start from priority institutions, then map relevant people and warm paths.
  • Separate public content from fund-specific materials. Public LinkedIn activity should stay thesis-led and educational.
  • Let the two profiles carry different signals. Kris leads institutional conviction; Zeynep opens ecosystem and relationship context.
  • Route interest into controlled next steps. Qualified interest should move to email, a meeting, or an eligibility-gated investor flow.

Allocator playbooks

Switch the angle by institution type.

The same quantum narrative will not work equally across each allocator category. Use the selector to keep the message specific before Kris or Zeynep starts engaging.

Family offices

Private conviction before formal process.

Family offices often respond to discreet, high-conviction conversations where the manager can frame the category without overselling it. Prioritize principals, CIOs, investment directors, and trusted advisors.

  • Lead with why quantum is becoming institutionally legible now.
  • Use Kris for conviction and Zeynep for warm bridges through events or shared networks.
  • Offer a short non-confidential investor note before asking for a meeting.

Outbound sequence

Manual touches, measured restraint.

No automation, no scraping, no bulk connection waves. The sequence below keeps LinkedIn native, human, and appropriate for senior allocator audiences.

Day 0

Research

Confirm the account, role, investment remit, recent posts, mutual connections, and likely relevance to quantum or venture allocation.

Day 1-5

Visible value

Follow the person, comment once or twice with a specific observation, and share a public post that answers a question they may already have.

Day 6-10

Connection

Send a short connection request tied to their role or recent topic. Do not include fund terms, performance language, or a hard meeting ask.

Day 11-16

Value note

After acceptance, send one useful note: a non-confidential insight, event takeaway, or allocator question. Ask whether comparing notes would be useful.

Day 17-24

Qualification

If they respond, move the conversation to email or a meeting and use the investor eligibility flow before sharing any fund-specific materials.

Day 30+

Nurture

If there is no reply, stop pushing. Keep light public engagement and revisit only when there is a real event, article, or introduction reason.

Optional paid support

Use paid LinkedIn as air cover.

Paid activity should amplify credibility around named accounts rather than replace manual relationship work. Start with account lists, document ads, video views, and conservative retargeting where consent and policy allow.

  • Company list targeting: Upload priority family office, FoF, endowment, and pension accounts, then refine by seniority and job function where audience size allows.
  • Document ads: Promote a short non-confidential "Quantum allocator note" from Kris, with a soft CTA to request information.
  • Video retargeting: Use short clips from Kris or Zeynep explaining one allocator question, then retarget engaged viewers with the investor note.
  • Lead Gen Forms: Use only if privacy policy, eligibility language, and compliance review are ready. Keep forms short and qualification-led.
  • UK targeting: Treat financial services verification as a launch dependency before any UK-targeted financial services ads.

Guardrails and sources

Keep the motion clean.

This page is a marketing strategy, not legal advice. Before launch, every profile claim, post template, ad, landing page, and investor handoff should be reviewed against Firgun's regulatory posture and the relevant jurisdiction.

  • No offer language in public posts. Keep public content educational, thesis-led, and balanced.
  • No performance promises. Avoid return expectations, guarantees, or urgency framing.
  • No automation or scraping. Manual work is slower, but it protects account integrity and trust.
  • No fund-specific materials before eligibility gating. Move serious interest into a controlled investor flow.
  • No duplicate outreach. Kris and Zeynep should share one tracker and one owner per relationship.
